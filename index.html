Building-scale functional mixing reveals a decarbonization window in Chinese cities

Jiayi Li, Zhen Zhang, Xin Huang, Xiaofeng Pan, Song Ma, Xiangyun Xiong, Liangpei Zhang
Wuhan University · Shenzhen Ecological & Environmental Monitoring Center · LIESMARS

About the study

Mixed land use is widely promoted as a route to urban decarbonization, yet its benefits remain poorly quantified because mixing is typically measured with coarse proxies that cannot resolve individual buildings — the units that organize energy use and mobility demand. This study builds the first nationwide, building-resolved functional atlas for China (~200 million buildings), enabled by sub-meter (0.5 m) remote-sensing imagery integrated with semantic and vector context.

Using a spatial econometric design that recovers non-linearity and spatial spillovers, the study quantifies how mixing at neighborhood versus building resolution relates to grid-level fossil-fuel operational CO₂. The analysis reveals a tri-phasic friction–synergy–saturation response with a bounded synergy window (BFMI ≈ 0.14–0.72), implying a national mitigation potential on the order of 10² Mt CO₂ yr⁻¹.

What this page shows

Part ABuilding function atlas

An interactive view of the nationwide atlas. Each building (or aggregated grid cell at coarse zoom) carries a two-tier functional label: BF1 — the contextual label, capturing the dominant land-use setting and planning intent of the surrounding district; and BF2 — the intrinsic label, capturing the building's own dominant function.

Zoom out (z 6.5–11) to see the nationwide Building Functional Mixing Index (BFMI) at grid scale — already normalised to [0, 1], where higher values indicate stronger intra-cell functional mixing. The bounded synergy window identified in the paper sits at BFMI ≈ 0.14–0.72. Zoom in (z 12–14) to inspect individual building footprints, where contextual and intrinsic labels can diverge — a dormitory inside an educational campus, ground-floor commerce under upper-floor residences, or housing embedded in an industrial park. Use the render-mode selector to color buildings by BF1 alone, or by the joint BF1+BF2 combination that drives the analysis.

Part BManual accuracy validation

To quantify how well the model recovers building function in the wild, 1,981 buildings drawn from a stratified national sample were manually inspected against sub-meter imagery and assigned reference labels BF1v and BF2v. The map below shows every validation point, colored by the human-checked label. Click a point to compare the model prediction (BF1, BF2) against the manual reference (BF1v, BF2v). Provinces are tinted by their overall accuracy (OA) and can be clicked to reveal the per-province precision, recall, F1-score and Cohen's Kappa.
